Uhlenbeck's trick: the statement
The evolution of curvature has the following ugly expression: \[\partial_t R_{ijkl}=\Delta R_{ikjl} + 2(B_{ijkl}-B_{ijlk}-B_{iljk}+B_{ikjl})-(R^p_iR_{pjkl}+R^p_j{ipkl}+R^p_kR_{ijpl}+R^p_lR_{ijkp}).\] While one can simplify it with the \(*\) notation and write \[(\partial_t-\Delta) Rm=Rm * Rm-Rc * Rm\] instead, it is actually doing nothing. Alternatively, let \((V,h)\) be a vector bundle and \(u_0:(V,h)\rightarrow (TM, g_0)\) be a bundle isometry. Observe that \(u(t): (V,h)\rightarrow (TM, g(t))\) given by \[ \begin{align} u(0)&=u_0;\\ \partial_t u^i_a &= R^i_lu^l_a \end{align} \] remains an isometry along the flow, and \[\partial_t R_{abcd}=\Delta R_{abcd} +2(B_{abcd}-B_{abdc}+B_{acbd}-B_{adbc}).\]
